Chapter 5 – Democratic Rights

What are Rights?

Rights are reasonable claims of individuals recognized by society and sanctioned by law. They are essential for the sustenance of democracy.

Why do we need Rights in a Democracy?

  • Protect individuals from arbitrary government actions.
  • Enable people to express themselves and form associations.
  • Allow participation in government and safeguard minorities.

Fundamental Rights in the Indian Constitution

  • Right to Equality: Equal treatment before law, no discrimination on grounds of religion, caste, gender, etc.
  • Right to Freedom: Includes freedom of speech, expression, movement, and right to life and liberty.
  • Right against Exploitation: Prohibits human trafficking, forced labor, and child labor under 14.
  • Right to Freedom of Religion: Freedom to practice, profess, and propagate any religion.
  • Cultural and Educational Rights: Rights of minorities to preserve their culture and establish educational institutions.
  • Right to Constitutional Remedies: Allows individuals to approach the court if their fundamental rights are violated.

Expanding Scope of Rights

The Supreme Court has interpreted the Right to Life to include right to food, clean environment, education, etc. Rights have expanded through judicial interpretation and public interest litigations (PIL).

Conclusion

Rights are the cornerstone of democracy. In India, fundamental rights empower citizens and ensure justice, equality, and dignity for all.
