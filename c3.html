Chapter 3 – Electoral Politics

Why Elections?

  • It is a mechanism to choose representatives by the people.
  • Ensures that leaders are accountable to the people.

What Makes an Election Democratic?

  • Free and fair elections
  • Universal adult franchise (18+ years)
  • Regular elections
  • Choice of candidates and parties

System of Elections in India

  • India has a parliamentary form of government.
  • Lok Sabha and State Assemblies are elected every 5 years.
  • Single-member constituencies with first-past-the-post system.

Election Process

  • Delimitation of constituencies
  • Preparation of voters’ list
  • Announcement of election schedule
  • Filing of nominations
  • Election campaign
  • Polling and counting of votes

Independent Election Commission

  • Conducts and oversees elections in India.
  • Ensures free and fair elections.
  • Has powers to implement code of conduct and punish offenders.

Challenges to Free and Fair Elections

  • Misuse of government machinery
  • Money and muscle power
  • Criminal background of candidates
  • Lack of internal democracy in political parties

Reforms Suggested

  • More power to Election Commission
  • Transparency in funding and candidate backgrounds
  • Reducing influence of money and caste in elections
