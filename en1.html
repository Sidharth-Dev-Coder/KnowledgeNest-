Class 9 English Integrated Grammar Notes

1. Tenses

Tenses show the time of action. There are 3 main tenses:

  • Present: I write.
  • Past: I wrote.
  • Future: I will write.

2. Modals

Modals are helping verbs (can, could, may, might, must, shall, should, will, would) used to express ability, possibility, permission, or obligation.

Examples: You should study hard. He might come today.

3. Subject-Verb Concord

The verb must agree with the subject in number and person.

Examples: She runs fast. They run fast.

4. Reported Speech

It is used to report what someone said.

Example: Direct: He said, "I am happy."
Indirect: He said that he was happy.

5. Determiners

Words placed before nouns to clarify them. (a, an, the, this, those, some, many)

Examples: Some people are missing. The sun is hot.

6. Prepositions

They show the relationship of a noun/pronoun with another word.

Examples: The book is on the table. He lives in Delhi.

7. Articles

‘A’ and ‘An’ are indefinite, and ‘The’ is a definite article.

Examples: I saw a dog. The moon is bright.

8. Active and Passive Voice

Active: Subject performs the action.
Passive: Subject receives the action.

Example: Active: She wrote a letter.
Passive: A letter was written by her.

9. Clauses

A clause is a group of words with a subject and verb.

Example: I know that he is right.

10. Editing and Omission

Correcting grammar or removing unnecessary words.

Tip: Read carefully for tense, verb form, articles, etc.

11. Gap Filling

Fill blanks with suitable words (articles, modals, verbs).

12. Sentence Reordering

Rearrange jumbled words to form correct sentences.

Example: "the / boy / playing / is / football" → The boy is playing football.
