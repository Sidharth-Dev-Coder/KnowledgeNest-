Chapter 2 – Constitutional Design

Need for a Constitution

A constitution provides a set of basic rules and principles for the functioning of a country. It reflects the aspirations of the people and ensures justice, liberty, and equality.

Making of the Indian Constitution

  • India got independence on 15th August 1947.
  • The Constitution came into effect on 26th January 1950.
  • The Constitution was drafted by the Constituent Assembly formed in 1946.
  • Dr. B.R. Ambedkar was the Chairman of the Drafting Committee.

Role of the Constituent Assembly

  • The Assembly had 299 members.
  • It worked for 3 years (from 1946 to 1949).
  • It reflected the will of the people and included all communities and regions.

Features of the Indian Constitution

  • Lengthiest written Constitution in the world
  • Federal system with unitary features
  • Parliamentary system of government
  • Secular and democratic republic
  • Universal adult franchise
  • Fundamental Rights and Duties
  • Directive Principles of State Policy

Philosophy of the Constitution

The values and vision of the Constitution are reflected in the Preamble.

Preamble of the Indian Constitution

"We, the people of India, having solemnly resolved to constitute India into a Sovereign Socialist Secular Democratic Republic and to secure to all its citizens: Justice, Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity..."
