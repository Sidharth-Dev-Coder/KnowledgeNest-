Chapter 9: Circles

Introduction

A circle is a collection of points in a plane that are at a fixed distance (radius) from a fixed point (centre).

Key Terms

  • Radius: Distance from centre to any point on the circle.
  • Diameter: A chord passing through the centre (2 × radius).
  • Chord: A line segment with both endpoints on the circle.
  • Arc: A portion of the circumference.
  • Segment: Region between a chord and the arc.
  • Sectors: Area enclosed between two radii and an arc.

Important Results

  • Equal chords of a circle subtend equal angles at the centre.
  • If the angles subtended by two chords at the centre are equal, then the chords are equal.
  • Perpendicular drawn from the centre to a chord bisects the chord.
  • Chords equidistant from the centre are equal in length.
  • The angle subtended by an arc at the centre is double the angle subtended on the remaining circle.
  • Angle in a semicircle is a right angle.
  • Angles in the same segment of a circle are equal.
  • Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary.

Theorems

  • Equal chords subtend equal angles at the centre.
  • Angle subtended by a diameter at the circle is 90°.
  • If a line segment joining two points subtends equal angles at two other points on the same side, then the four points lie on a circle (cyclic).

Summary

Circles are not only simple shapes but are fundamental to geometry. This chapter introduces key concepts of chords, arcs, angles, and their relations to form cyclic figures. These concepts are useful in real-world designs, construction, and geometry problems.
