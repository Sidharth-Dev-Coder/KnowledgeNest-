Chapter 2 – People as Resource

Introduction

People as resource is a way of referring to a country’s working people in terms of their existing productive skills and abilities. It emphasizes investing in human capital through education, health, and skill development.

Economic Activities

Economic activities are those activities that add value to the national income. These can be:

  • Market activities: Involve remuneration to anyone who performs them (paid work).
  • Non-market activities: Production for self-consumption.

Quality of Population

The quality of a population depends on literacy rate, health of a person, and skill formation. These factors enhance the productivity of the population and contribute to the economic growth of the country.

Education

Education is one of the most important factors in human capital formation. It improves knowledge, skill, and efficiency of people, thereby contributing to better productivity and income.

Health

Healthier individuals are more efficient and productive. Investment in health includes providing better medical facilities, nutrition, and sanitation.

Unemployment

Unemployment refers to the situation where people who are willing and able to work at the prevailing wage rate cannot find jobs. Types of unemployment include:

  • Seasonal unemployment: Found in agricultural laborers during the off-season.
  • Disguised unemployment: More people are employed than actually needed, common in rural areas.

Conclusion

Treating people as a resource is a positive approach towards development. Human capital, when nurtured through proper investment in education and health, becomes an asset to the nation.
