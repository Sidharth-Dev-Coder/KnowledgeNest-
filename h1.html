Chapter 1 – The French Revolution

Introduction

The French Revolution began in 1789 and ended in the late 1790s. It marked the end of monarchy and paved the way for democracy and modern nation-states.

Causes of the French Revolution

  • Social Inequality: French society was divided into three estates. The First and Second Estates enjoyed privileges while the Third Estate bore the burden of taxes.
  • Economic Crisis: France faced debt due to lavish spending and wars, especially the American War of Independence.
  • Enlightenment Ideas: Philosophers like Rousseau and Voltaire inspired people with ideas of liberty and equality.
  • Weak Monarchy: King Louis XVI was indecisive and could not handle the crisis effectively.

The Outbreak of the Revolution

On 5th May 1789, Louis XVI called a meeting of the Estates-General. The Third Estate demanded a vote per person, which was denied. On 14th July 1789, the Bastille was stormed, symbolizing the start of the revolution.

End of Monarchy

  • France became a constitutional monarchy in 1791.
  • In 1792, monarchy was abolished and France became a republic.
  • In 1793, Louis XVI was executed.

The Reign of Terror

From 1793 to 1794, Robespierre ruled France with strict laws and severe punishments. Thousands were executed. Eventually, Robespierre was also executed and this period ended.

The Rise of Napoleon

In 1799, Napoleon Bonaparte took control through a coup. He introduced many reforms but later became Emperor. He was finally defeated at the Battle of Waterloo in 1815.

Legacy of the French Revolution

  • Spread of ideas of liberty, equality, and fraternity.
  • Inspired revolutions across Europe and the world.
  • End of feudalism and emergence of democratic ideals.
