Chapter 12: Statistics

Introduction

Statistics is the branch of mathematics that deals with collection, classification, analysis, and interpretation of numerical data.

Important Terms

  • Data: Collection of numerical facts or observations.
  • Primary Data: Data collected by the investigator directly.
  • Secondary Data: Data collected from a source already available.

Presentation of Data

  • Ungrouped Data: Data in raw form (no grouping).
  • Grouped Data: Data arranged in groups (class intervals).
  • Frequency: Number of times an observation occurs.

Types of Frequency Distribution

  • Discrete Frequency Distribution
  • Continuous Frequency Distribution

Measures of Central Tendency

The three most commonly used measures are:

  • Mean (Average)
  • Median (Middle value)
  • Mode (Most frequent value)

1. Mean

For ungrouped data:
Mean = (Sum of observations) / (Number of observations)

For grouped data:
Mean = (Σfx) / (Σf)
Where f is frequency and x is mid-point of each class.

2. Median

For ungrouped data:
Arrange data in ascending order and find the middle value.

3. Mode

The value which occurs most frequently in the data.

Graphical Representation

  • Bar Graph
  • Histogram
  • Frequency Polygon

Example

Find the mean of: 10, 20, 30, 40, 50
Mean = (10+20+30+40+50)/5 = 150/5 = 30

Summary

  • Statistics helps in organizing and analyzing data.
  • Mean, median, and mode are key concepts.
  • Data can be shown in tables and graphs for better understanding.
