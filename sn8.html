Chapter 8: Motion

Introduction

Motion refers to the change in position of an object with time. Objects can move in different ways such as straight line, circular or oscillatory motion.

Types of Motion

  • Uniform Motion: Object moves with constant speed in a straight line.
  • Non-uniform Motion: Object moves with changing speed or direction.

Distance and Displacement

  • Distance: Total path covered by an object (scalar).
  • Displacement: Shortest distance from initial to final point (vector).

Speed

  • Speed = Distance / Time
  • SI unit: m/s

Velocity

  • Velocity = Displacement / Time
  • SI unit: m/s
  • Velocity is speed with direction.

Acceleration

  • Acceleration = Change in velocity / Time
  • SI unit: m/s²
  • Can be positive (speeding up) or negative (slowing down).

Equations of Uniformly Accelerated Motion

  • v = u + at
  • s = ut + ½at²
  • v² = u² + 2as
  • Where:
    u = initial velocity
    v = final velocity
    a = acceleration
    s = displacement
    t = time

Graphical Representation of Motion

  • Distance-Time Graph: Shows the distance travelled over time. A straight line indicates uniform motion.
  • Velocity-Time Graph: The slope of this graph gives acceleration. The area under the graph gives displacement.

Conclusion

Understanding motion helps us describe and predict how objects move, and is the foundation for mechanics in physics.
