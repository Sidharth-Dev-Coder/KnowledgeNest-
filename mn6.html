Chapter 6: Lines and Angles

Introduction

This chapter deals with the basic concepts related to lines, line segments, rays, and angles. It forms the foundation of Geometry.

Basic Terms and Definitions

  • Line: A straight one-dimensional figure that has no thickness and extends infinitely in both directions.
  • Line Segment: A part of a line that is bounded by two distinct end points.
  • Ray: A part of a line that starts at a point and extends infinitely in one direction.
  • Angle: Formed when two rays originate from the same endpoint.

Types of Angles

  • Acute Angle: less than 90°
  • Right Angle: exactly 90°
  • Obtuse Angle: between 90° and 180°
  • Straight Angle: exactly 180°
  • Reflex Angle: more than 180°

Pair of Angles

  • Complementary Angles: Sum is 90°
  • Supplementary Angles: Sum is 180°
  • Adjacent Angles: Share a common vertex and side
  • Linear Pair: Adjacent and supplementary
  • Vertically Opposite Angles: Formed when two lines intersect, and are equal

Intersecting Lines and Non-Intersecting Lines

Two lines that cross each other at a point are intersecting lines. If they never meet, they are non-intersecting or parallel lines.

Parallel Lines and a Transversal

A line that cuts two or more lines at distinct points is called a transversal. When a transversal intersects two parallel lines, several angles are formed.

Angles Formed by a Transversal

  • Corresponding Angles (equal)
  • Alternate Interior Angles (equal)
  • Alternate Exterior Angles (equal)
  • Consecutive Interior Angles (supplementary)

Parallel Line Criteria

If corresponding angles, alternate interior angles, or alternate exterior angles are equal, then the lines are parallel.

Summary

This chapter introduces the fundamental concepts of lines, angles, and their relationships. It builds a strong base for future geometry studies.
