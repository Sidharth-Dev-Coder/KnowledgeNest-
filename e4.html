Chapter 4 – Food Security in India

Introduction

Food security means availability, accessibility, and affordability of food to all people at all times. It is essential for the overall well-being and health of a nation’s population.

What is Food Security?

  • Availability: Sufficient food production in the country.
  • Accessibility: Food is within the reach of every person.
  • Affordability: Individuals have enough income to buy food.

Why Food Security?

  • To ensure people do not suffer from hunger or malnutrition.
  • During natural disasters like floods, droughts, or pandemics.
  • For a stable and healthy society.

Who are Food-Insecure People?

  • Landless rural laborers
  • Urban slum dwellers
  • SCs, STs, and backward communities
  • Pregnant women and children
  • People affected by natural calamities or displacement

Food Security in India

India became self-sufficient in food grains due to the Green Revolution. The government has adopted various strategies like buffer stock and Public Distribution System (PDS) to maintain food security.

Buffer Stock

It refers to the stock of food grains maintained by the government to be used during shortage. It is maintained by the Food Corporation of India (FCI).

Public Distribution System (PDS)

PDS distributes food and other essentials at subsidized prices through Fair Price Shops (ration shops). It includes:

  • Ration cards: For identifying beneficiaries.
  • TPDS: Targeted Public Distribution System introduced in 1997 for BPL families.

Other Poverty Alleviation Programmes

  • Antyodaya Anna Yojana (AAY)
  • National Food for Work Programme (NFWP)
  • Mid-Day Meal Scheme (MDMS)

Problems with PDS

  • Leakage of food grains
  • Poor quality of food
  • Corruption and black marketing

Conclusion

Food security is not just about food availability but also ensuring it reaches the needy. Strengthening infrastructure, removing corruption, and increasing awareness are crucial for long-term success.
