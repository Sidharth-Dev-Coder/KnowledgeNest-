Geography - Chapter 3: Drainage

1. What is Drainage?

Drainage refers to the river system of an area. A drainage basin is the area drained by a single river system.

2. Major Drainage Systems in India

  • Himalayan Rivers: Perennial (fed by snow and rain). E.g. Ganga, Indus, Brahmaputra.
  • Peninsular Rivers: Seasonal (dependent on rainfall). E.g. Godavari, Krishna, Mahanadi.

3. The Himalayan River Systems

  • Indus: Originates in Tibet, flows through Ladakh and Pakistan.
  • Ganga: Originates at Gangotri glacier, joined by Yamuna, Son, Gandak.
  • Brahmaputra: Known as Tsangpo in Tibet, enters Arunachal Pradesh as Dihang.

4. Peninsular Rivers

  • Godavari: Longest Peninsular river, called Dakshin Ganga.
  • Other major rivers: Mahanadi, Krishna, Kaveri (east-flowing).
  • West-flowing rivers: Narmada and Tapi flow into Arabian Sea.

5. Lakes

  • Natural Lakes: Wular, Dal, Bhimtal, Nainital.
  • Artificial Lakes: Created by dams, e.g. Govind Sagar (on Sutlej).

6. Importance of Rivers

Rivers are crucial for irrigation, drinking water, industries, hydroelectricity, and transportation.

7. River Pollution

Domestic and industrial waste is polluting rivers. The Ganga and Yamuna are severely affected.
