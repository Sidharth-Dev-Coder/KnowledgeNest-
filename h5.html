Chapter 5 – Pastoralists in the Modern World

Introduction

This chapter explores the lives of pastoral communities who depend on animal herding for livelihood. It shows how colonial policies affected them in India and Africa.

Pastoral Communities in India

Pastoralists lived in various regions like the mountains, plateaus, and deserts. They moved from one place to another in search of pasture and water for their animals.

Types of Indian Pastoralists

  • Gujjars in Jammu and Garhwal
  • Gaddis in Himachal Pradesh
  • Banjaras in Uttar Pradesh
  • Maldharis in Gujarat
  • Dhangars in Maharashtra

Colonial Impact on Pastoralists

British policies changed the lives of pastoralists significantly:

  • Wasteland rules – marked grazing land as government property
  • Forest Acts – restricted access to forest resources
  • Criminal Tribes Act – labeled many pastoral groups as criminals
  • Taxation – imposed grazing taxes and increased financial burden

Adaptation and Resistance

  • Some settled permanently and took to agriculture
  • Others migrated to new regions or changed routes
  • Some resisted through protests and petitions

Pastoralism in Africa

African pastoral groups like the Maasai were also affected by European colonialism. Their grazing lands were taken over and they were forced into reserves.

Effects of Colonial Rule in Africa

  • Land alienation – best grazing lands taken by European settlers
  • Restriction on movement
  • Taxation and enforced labor

Conclusion

Colonial policies caused major disruptions in the lives of pastoralists, both in India and Africa. Despite this, many communities adapted in new ways to survive.
