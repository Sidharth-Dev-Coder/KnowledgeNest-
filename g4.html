Geography - Chapter 4: Climate

1. Introduction

India’s climate refers to the average weather condition over a long period. It is classified as Monsoon type climate.

2. Factors Affecting Climate

  • Latitude: India’s location in the tropical and subtropical zone affects temperature.
  • Altitude: Places at higher altitudes like Shimla are cooler.
  • Pressure and Winds: Surface pressure, wind system, and upper air circulation all affect climate.
  • Distance from the Sea: Places closer to the sea have moderate climate (e.g., Mumbai).
  • Relief: The Himalayas prevent cold winds from Central Asia.

3. Indian Monsoon

The monsoon is the most important feature of India's climate. The reversal of wind system leads to monsoon. It is seasonal, caused by differential heating of land and sea.

4. Seasons in India

  • Cold Weather Season (Winter): December to February.
  • Hot Weather Season (Summer): March to May.
  • South-West Monsoon Season: June to September.
  • Retreating Monsoon (Transition Season): October and November.

5. Monsoon Mechanism

The high pressure over Indian Ocean and low pressure over the land during summer causes the moist winds to blow from sea to land bringing rainfall.

6. Distribution of Rainfall

  • Heavy rainfall areas: Meghalaya, Western Ghats, Northeast.
  • Moderate rainfall areas: Indo-Gangetic plain.
  • Low rainfall areas: Rajasthan, parts of Gujarat, Leh.

7. Importance of Monsoon

Agriculture in India depends on monsoon. It affects water supply, electricity generation, and economic activities.
