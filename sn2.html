Chapter 2 – Is Matter Around Us Pure

Pure Substances

A pure substance consists of a single type of particle. It cannot be separated into other substances by physical means.

Mixtures

A mixture contains two or more substances (elements or compounds) mixed together in any ratio. They are not chemically combined.

  • Homogeneous mixture: Uniform composition throughout (e.g., salt in water).
  • Heterogeneous mixture: Non-uniform composition (e.g., sand in water).

Solutions

A homogeneous mixture of two or more substances.

  • Solute: The substance dissolved.
  • Solvent: The substance in which solute is dissolved.

Properties: Stable, clear, cannot be separated by filtration.

Types of Solutions

  • Solid in liquid – Sugar in water
  • Gas in liquid – Oxygen in water
  • Liquid in liquid – Alcohol in water

Suspensions

A heterogeneous mixture in which particles are large enough to settle or be filtered out.

  • Can be seen with naked eye
  • Unstable and can be separated by filtration

Colloids

A mixture with intermediate particle size, appearing homogeneous but actually heterogeneous.

  • Particles do not settle
  • Show Tyndall effect
  • Cannot be separated by filtration

Separation of Mixtures

  • Evaporation: Separates a soluble solid from liquid (e.g., salt from water)
  • Filtration: For insoluble solids in liquids
  • Distillation: For separating miscible liquids with different boiling points
  • Centrifugation: Separates suspended particles in liquids
  • Chromatography: Separates colors or components in ink

Physical and Chemical Changes

  • Physical change: No new substance formed, reversible (e.g., melting of ice)
  • Chemical change: New substance formed, irreversible (e.g., burning of paper)

Elements, Compounds and Mixtures

  • Element: Made of same kind of atoms (e.g., Hydrogen)
  • Compound: Two or more elements chemically combined (e.g., H₂O)
  • Mixture: Two or more substances mixed physically (e.g., air)

Conclusion

This chapter helps us understand how substances around us are either pure or mixtures, and how we can separate mixtures using physical methods.
