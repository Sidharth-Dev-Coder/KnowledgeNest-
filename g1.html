Chapter 1 – India: Size and Location

Location

India is located in the Northern Hemisphere and Eastern Hemisphere. It lies between:

  • Latitudes: 8°4'N to 37°6'N
  • Longitudes: 68°7'E to 97°25'E

The Tropic of Cancer (23°30'N) divides India into two almost equal halves.

Size

  • India is the 7th largest country in the world (land area: 3.28 million sq km).
  • It has a land boundary of about 15,200 km and a coastline of 7,516.6 km.

India and the World

  • India’s location at the head of the Indian Ocean gives it a strategic central position in Asia.
  • It has strong trade relations through both land and sea routes with countries of the East and West.

India’s Neighbours

  • India shares its borders with Pakistan, Afghanistan, China, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar.
  • It shares maritime boundaries with Sri Lanka and Maldives.

Significance of India’s Location

  • Favorable for trade with countries in East Africa, West Asia, and Southeast Asia.
  • Central location between East and West Asia enhances strategic and cultural contact.

Conclusion

India’s size and geographical location have contributed greatly to its rich cultural contacts, trade routes, and strategic importance throughout history.
