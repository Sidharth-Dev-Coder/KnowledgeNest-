Chapter 11: Surface Areas and Volumes

Introduction

In this chapter, we study the surface areas and volumes of solid shapes such as cubes, cuboids, cylinders, cones, spheres, and hemispheres.

Basic Concepts

  • Surface Area: Total area of all the faces (outer surface) of a 3D object.
  • Volume: Space occupied by a 3D object.

Formulas for Surface Area and Volume

1. Cuboid

  • Surface Area = 2(lb + bh + hl)
  • Volume = l × b × h

2. Cube

  • Surface Area = 6a²
  • Volume = a³

3. Cylinder

  • Curved Surface Area = 2πrh
  • Total Surface Area = 2πr(h + r)
  • Volume = πr²h

4. Cone

  • Curved Surface Area = πrl
  • Total Surface Area = πr(l + r)
  • Volume = (1/3)πr²h

5. Sphere

  • Surface Area = 4πr²
  • Volume = (4/3)πr³

6. Hemisphere

  • Curved Surface Area = 2πr²
  • Total Surface Area = 3πr²
  • Volume = (2/3)πr³

Applications

  • Finding cost of painting or wrapping objects (uses surface area).
  • Finding capacity or storage space (uses volume).

Conversion of Solids

Sometimes one shape is melted and recast into another. In such cases:

Volume remains constant

Volume of original solid = Volume of new solid

Summary

  • Learn and memorize all formulas.
  • Surface area deals with outer surface; volume deals with inner capacity.
  • Always use correct units: cm² for area, cm³ for volume.
