Class 9 Science - Chapter 1: Matter in Our Surroundings

1. What is Matter?

Anything that has mass and occupies space. Everything around us—air, water, books, etc.—is matter.

2. States of Matter

State Shape Volume Examples
Solid Fixed Fixed Ice, Wood
Liquid Variable Fixed Water, Oil
Gas Variable Variable Air, Steam

3. Change of State

  • Melting: Solid → Liquid
  • Freezing: Liquid → Solid
  • Evaporation: Liquid → Gas
  • Condensation: Gas → Liquid
  • Sublimation: Solid ↔ Gas

4. Evaporation

  • Cooling process
  • Faster with more surface area, heat, wind
  • Used in drying clothes, cooling water in earthen pots

5. Why Evaporation Causes Cooling?

High-energy particles escape first, reducing the average energy of remaining particles, lowering temperature.

6. Characteristics of Particles of Matter

  • Tiny and invisible
  • Have space between them
  • Move continuously
  • Attract each other

7. Summary

This chapter explains the nature of matter, its states, properties, and how physical changes like evaporation, sublimation, and boiling work.
