Chapter 10: Heron’s Formula

Introduction

In this chapter, we learn how to find the area of a triangle using Heron’s Formula, especially when all three sides are known and height is not given.

Heron’s Formula

Let the sides of a triangle be a, b, and c.

  • Step 1: Calculate semi-perimeter (s):
    s = (a + b + c) / 2
  • Step 2: Use the formula:
    Area = √[s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c)]

Applications

  • Area of scalene triangles where no height is given
  • Area of quadrilaterals by dividing them into two triangles

Examples

Example: Find the area of a triangle with sides 7 cm, 8 cm, and 9 cm.

  • s = (7 + 8 + 9)/2 = 12
  • Area = √[12(12-7)(12-8)(12-9)] = √[12×5×4×3] = √720 ≈ 26.83 cm²

Area of Quadrilaterals

If a quadrilateral is divided into two triangles, Heron’s formula can be used for each triangle separately and then their areas can be added.

Important Points

  • Only applicable when all sides are known.
  • Don’t forget to find semi-perimeter first.
  • Ensure the triangle is valid before applying the formula.

Summary

Heron’s Formula is useful in calculating the area of triangles and some quadrilaterals when perpendicular height is not available. It is based on the semi-perimeter and all three side lengths.
