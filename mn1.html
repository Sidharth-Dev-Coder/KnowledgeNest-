Chapter 1: Number Systems

Topics Covered:

  • Real Numbers
  • Irrational Numbers
  • Representation on Number Line
  • Operations on Real Numbers
  • Laws of Exponents

1. Real Numbers

The collection of rational and irrational numbers is called real numbers. Every point on the number line represents a unique real number.

2. Irrational Numbers

  • Numbers that cannot be expressed in the form of p/q where q ≠ 0.
  • Examples: √2, π, √3, etc.

3. Representation of √2 on the Number Line

Using Pythagoras theorem, draw a right-angled triangle with base 1 unit and height 1 unit, then use compass to represent √2 on number line.

4. Operations on Real Numbers

  • Closure: Real numbers are closed under addition, subtraction, multiplication and division (except by 0).
  • Commutative: a + b = b + a and a × b = b × a
  • Associative: (a + b) + c = a + (b + c)
  • Distributive: a × (b + c) = ab + ac

5. Laws of Exponents for Real Numbers

  • am × an = am+n
  • (am)n = amn
  • am/an = am-n
  • a0 = 1 (a ≠ 0)
  • (ab)m = am × bm

Conclusion

This chapter forms the foundation of number theory and helps understand how different types of numbers behave under various operations.
