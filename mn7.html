Chapter 7: Triangles

Introduction

This chapter introduces the concept of triangles, their properties, rules of congruence, and theorems related to triangles.

Triangles

A triangle is a closed figure formed by three line segments. It has three sides, three angles, and three vertices.

Types of Triangles

  • Based on Sides:
    • Scalene: All sides unequal
    • Isosceles: Two sides equal
    • Equilateral: All sides equal
  • Based on Angles:
    • Acute-angled: All angles less than 90°
    • Right-angled: One angle exactly 90°
    • Obtuse-angled: One angle more than 90°

Congruence of Triangles

Two triangles are congruent if all their corresponding sides and angles are equal.

Congruence Criteria

  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side)
  • ASA (Angle-Side-Angle)
  • SSS (Side-Side-Side)
  • RHS (Right angle-Hypotenuse-Side)

Properties of Triangle

  • Angles opposite to equal sides are equal.
  • Sides opposite to equal angles are equal.

Triangle Inequality Theorem

The sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle is always greater than the length of the third side.

Angle Sum Property

The sum of the interior angles of a triangle is always 180°.

Summary

This chapter helps build understanding of triangles, their types, congruence criteria, and important properties which are useful in further geometry.
