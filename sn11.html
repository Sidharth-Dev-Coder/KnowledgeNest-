Chapter 11: Work and Energy

What is Work?

  • Work is said to be done when a force is applied on an object and it displaces in the direction of the force.
  • Work (W) = Force × Displacement × cosθ
  • SI Unit: Joule (J)
  • If displacement is zero, work done is zero.

Types of Work

  • Positive Work: When force and displacement are in the same direction.
  • Negative Work: When force and displacement are in opposite direction.
  • Zero Work: No displacement or force is perpendicular to displacement.

Energy

Energy is the capacity to do work.

  • SI Unit: Joule (J)
  • Common unit: kilojoule (kJ)

Forms of Energy

  • Mechanical Energy (Kinetic + Potential)
  • Chemical Energy
  • Heat Energy
  • Light Energy
  • Sound Energy
  • Electrical Energy

Kinetic Energy

Energy possessed by a body due to its motion.

Formula: KE = ½ mv²

Potential Energy

Energy possessed by a body due to its position or configuration.

Formula: PE = mgh

Work-Energy Theorem

The work done by the net force on an object is equal to the change in its kinetic energy.

Law of Conservation of Energy

  • Energy can neither be created nor destroyed.
  • It can only be transformed from one form to another.
  • Total energy remains constant in an isolated system.

Power

  • Rate of doing work
  • Power = Work done / Time
  • SI unit: Watt (W)
  • 1 kilowatt (kW) = 1000 W

Commercial Unit of Energy

1 kilowatt hour (kWh) = 3.6 × 106 Joules
