Chapter 4 – Working of Institutions

Introduction

This chapter explains how various institutions of government work and coordinate to implement decisions in a democracy like India.

Need for Political Institutions

  • To take and implement decisions.
  • To resolve disputes.
  • To ensure welfare and justice.

Major Institutions

  • Parliament – Makes laws and controls the government.
  • Executive – Implements laws and policies.
  • Judiciary – Interprets laws and delivers justice.

Parliament

  • Consists of President, Lok Sabha, and Rajya Sabha.
  • Lok Sabha is more powerful and directly elected by the people.
  • Controls the executive through motions and questions.

Executive

  • Political Executive: Includes Prime Minister and Council of Ministers.
  • Permanent Executive: Bureaucracy that remains in service regardless of ruling party.
  • Real power is exercised by the political executive.

Prime Minister and Council of Ministers

  • PM is the head of government and leader of the majority party.
  • Appoints ministers and distributes portfolios.
  • Council of Ministers is collectively responsible to Lok Sabha.

President

  • Head of state and nominal executive.
  • Elected indirectly for 5 years.
  • Acts on the advice of the Council of Ministers.

Judiciary

  • Independent of executive and legislature.
  • Supreme Court is the highest court in India.
  • Can check the functioning of legislature and executive.
  • Ensures rule of law and protects fundamental rights.

Conclusion

Institutions are the backbone of democracy. They provide structure, ensure accountability, and protect the rights of citizens.
