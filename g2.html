Chapter 2 – Physical Features of India

Introduction

India’s landmass shows great physical diversity. Geologically, the country is divided into different landform types formed through various geological processes.

Major Physical Divisions of India

  1. The Himalayan Mountains
  2. The Northern Plains
  3. The Peninsular Plateau
  4. The Indian Desert
  5. The Coastal Plains
  6. The Islands

1. The Himalayan Mountains

  • Young fold mountains, formed by tectonic collision.
  • Three parallel ranges: Himadri (Greater Himalayas), Himachal (Middle Himalayas), Shiwalik (Outer Himalayas).
  • Important ranges: Karakoram, Zaskar, Ladakh, Pir Panjal.

2. The Northern Plains

  • Formed by the deposition of alluvium by the Indus, Ganga, and Brahmaputra rivers.
  • Divided into: Punjab Plains, Ganga Plains, Brahmaputra Plains.
  • Extremely fertile, densely populated, and agriculturally rich.

3. The Peninsular Plateau

  • Oldest landmass of India; formed by igneous and metamorphic rocks.
  • Divided into Central Highlands and Deccan Plateau.
  • Western Ghats and Eastern Ghats flank the Deccan Plateau.

4. The Indian Desert

  • Located in Rajasthan; known as the Thar Desert.
  • Arid climate, sandy soil, low rainfall.

5. The Coastal Plains

  • Narrow plains along the east and west coast of India.
  • Eastern Coastal Plain: between Eastern Ghats and Bay of Bengal.
  • Western Coastal Plain: between Western Ghats and Arabian Sea.

6. The Islands

  • Andaman and Nicobar Islands in Bay of Bengal – volcanic origin.
  • Lakshadweep Islands in Arabian Sea – coral origin.

Conclusion

The diverse physical features of India influence climate, vegetation, human settlements, and economic activities in different regions.
