Geography - Chapter 6: Population

1. Introduction

Population is a crucial component of social studies. It affects the resources, environment, and development of any country. India's population is diverse and dynamic.

2. Census

The census is the official count of population conducted every 10 years. It provides essential information like age, sex ratio, literacy rate, occupation, etc.

3. Population Size and Distribution

  • India is the second most populous country after China.
  • As per the 2011 Census, India’s population was over 1.21 billion.
  • Population is unevenly distributed – states like Uttar Pradesh are densely populated while Sikkim is sparsely populated.

4. Population Growth

  • Population growth refers to the change in population over time.
  • Two types:
    • Natural Growth: Difference between birth rate and death rate.
    • Actual Growth: Natural growth plus immigration minus emigration.

5. Population Density

It is the number of people living per square kilometer. As of 2011, India’s density was 382 persons/sq. km. Bihar has the highest density, while Arunachal Pradesh has the lowest.

6. Population Composition

  • Age Composition: Children (below 15), working population (15–59), aged (60+).
  • Sex Ratio: Number of females per 1000 males. India’s average is 940 (as per 2011).
  • Literacy Rate: 74% overall; higher in males than females.
  • Occupational Structure: Majority are engaged in primary sector like farming.

7. Adolescents in India

Adolescents (age 10–19) form about 20% of the population. Their education, nutrition, and development are crucial for national progress.

8. Health and National Population Policy

  • Health indicators include life expectancy, infant mortality, nutrition, etc.
  • National Population Policy 2000 aims to stabilize population, promote reproductive health, and improve health care infrastructure.

9. Conclusion

Understanding population trends helps in planning and development. Balanced population growth is essential for sustainable development.
